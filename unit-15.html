2. Climate change & pollution

Target: 80%

Vocabulary note

The prefix re- often tells us that something is being done again: reuse, revegetate.

The prefix de- often tells us that something is being removed: decaffeinated, deforestation.

Vocabulary note

The prefix ir- is often used with adjectives beginning with r to form the opposite or to mean lacking something: reversible, irreversible, regular, irregular. Some words beginning with r form their opposite with un-: realistic, unrealistic.

  3. It is (reasonable) for rich countries to expect developing countries to reduce carbon emissions immediately.

  6. Scientists believe that the damage to this area is (reversible).
