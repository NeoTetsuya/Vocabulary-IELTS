3. Media Adjectives

Target: 100%

Vocabulary Note

We say something has a good/bad/positive/negative influence on someone. (John's new friends are a really bad influence on him.)

Remember that effect is a noun and affect is a verb. (The media affects the way many people think. The media has a major effect on the way many people think.)

Error Warning! We use on with broadcast media (on the radio, on television, on screen, on the Internet). We use in with printed media (in magazines, in the press).

Test Practice

Academic Writing Task 2

You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.

Present a written argument or case to an educated reader with no specialist knowledge of the following topic.

Whoever controls the media also controls opinions and attitudes of the people and there is little that can be done to rectify this.

To what extent do you agree or disagree?

You should use your own ideas, knowledge and experience and support your arguments with examples and relevant evidence.
Write at least 250 words.

Test Tip

Use extra paper to make a plan before you begin to write so that you can organise your ideas logically. Do not try to learn any essays by heart. When you have finished, check your spelling and make sure you have written at least 250 words.
